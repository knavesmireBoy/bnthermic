BN Thermic Instruction Sheet

Heavy Duty Radiant Cassettes - TVH

INSTALLATION AND OPERATING INSTRUCTIONS

INCOMING INSPECTION

Check that the product has no visible damage from transportation.
Check that the product received has the correct output and voltage in relation to specified requirements.

APPROVAL / AREAS OF USE

TVH conforms to IP 55 and can normally be used in dry, moist and wet rooms.
Must not be used in explosive or flammable areas.
However, upon application to the local electricity inspection authority, installations in the latter two areas may receive approval.

POSITIONING

TVH must be mounted at least 1.8 meters above floor level. Distance to flammable building components: see figures for the various models.
Ceiling rails and ceiling brackets are supplied with the radiant heater, and can be used to mount the heater to the ceiling or fixture rail.
The distance to the wall or ceiling may be extended with the use of chains or wall brackets. These are supplied as extras.
When mounting TVH, the connection box must always be the lowest point. Do not mount the TVH low over permanent work places so that people are exposed to high temperatures are head level.
Do not mount TVH directly onto vinyl-covered ceiling panels, as the vinyl will be damaged even at low temperatures. Do not place TVH very close to large expanses of glass, as the glass may expand locally and crack.

MOUNTING TVH 3000

Mounting on enclosed ceiling rails and wall brackets.
Four screws for mounting TVH are supplied with the heater and enclosed in the connection box, along with four retention screws (with mounting instructions).

MOUNTING TVH 4500 and TVH 6000

Mounting on enclosed ceiling rails and wall brackets.
Four screws for mounting TVH are supplied with the heater and enclosed in the connection box, along with four retention screws (with mounting instructions).

MOUNTING TO CEILING

Mounting on enclosed ceiling rails and wall brackets.
Four screws for mounting TVH are supplied with the heater and enclosed in the connection box, along with four retention screws (with mounting instructions).

1. Screw enclosed ceiling rails onto ceiling.
The distance 134mm is the distance to the heater endplate.
The distance 207mm is the distance to the heater connection box.

2. Fit silicone cable to heater.

3. Attach enclosed wall brackets to heater.
(Attach with the M6 bolts supplied with the heater.)

4. Slide the heater onto the ceiling rails and secure with the PAN head screw A 3.5x13
and the PAN head screw C 3.5x19 supplied with the heater.

5. Connect heater to the mains.

ELECTRICAL INSTALLATION

The heater must only be installed by an authorised electrician. The heater is supplied with a 1-metre length of heat-resistant cable with a sealing nipple that must be used because of the extremely high temperatures that may arise.

CONNECTION OPTIONS

TVH 3000 AND TVH 4500 are fitted with three identical elements in the radiating surface.

400V/3ac

TVH 6000 is fitted with six identical elements in the radiating surface.

400/3ac

STARTING UP
Check that external and any internal cables are correctly connected up in accordance with the diagrams. Elements are to be in a star configuration for 3x400V, in a triangle configuration for 3x230V. The installation is to be Megger-tested between phases and ground to disclose any short-circuits. Resistance in the heater or circuit for several heaters is to be measured. The resistance is to be the same for all phases.
Output control in 3-phase installations:
P=2U2/R : P = output in watt, U = voltage, R = resistance in W.
Switch on power and check that switches, thermostats, etc, function properly.

Note! When switching the heater on for the first time, it is normal for there to be some smoke and smell as binding agents in the insulation burn off.
When the heater is switched off or on, some crackling sounds are normal. These are caused by the expansion or contraction of the elements.
When the heater is switched on, the radiating surface will normally buckle out about 1 cm; this will revert once the heater is switched off.

INSTRUCTIONS FOR USE.
The heater does not have built-in controls. Instructions for use must be drawn up based on the type of external control equipment used.

MAINTENANCE
The heater is maintenance-free, but it is particularly important that the top be cleaned.
Thick layers of dust on the apparatus represent a fire hazard.
The firm should draw up routines for cleaning the heaters.

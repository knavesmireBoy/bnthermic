BN Thermic Instruction Sheet

FFIH Floor Drop-In Heaters

INSTALLATION AND OPERATING INSTRUCTIONS

POSITIONING
The FFIH Heater is designed to be recessed into a concrete or wooden floor. It must not be installed in an area where the grill could be obstructed by doors, curtains or furniture. Do not install air outlet closer than I50mm from any wall or window. Do not cover the heater as this can cause overheating and may damage the unit and become a fire hazard. It must not be installed in the immediate surroundings of a bath, shower or swimming pool.

FIXING
Unpack the heater and remove all packing materials. Disassemble the heater. Cut an opening in the floor, 365 x 188, a minimum depth of 200mm is required. If a concrete floor is used set the recess box so that the flange will rest flush with the finished floor. Remove a convenient knock out, bush the hole if cable is used or prepare for a conduit entry if applicable, secure recess box in place after completing the wiring and leaving approximately 200mm of wire for termination. Install the heater in the box and remove the terminal cover - 2 screws. Connect the supply cable earth wire to the green earth terminal. The live wire usually red or brown to the wire marked LI. The neutral, usually black or blue to the wire marked N. After ensuring all electrical connections are tight, refit the terminal cover. This unit must be earthed. Make sure no foreign material is present and the fan is free to turn. Fit the protective grill. Finally fit the main grill using the 2 screws provided. Apply power to the heater to test. Sometimes a slight odour may be noticed, this is the protective coating of the element, it will disappear after a few moments of operation.

OPERATION
The heater is designed to be controlled by a manual switch or a room thermostat and the fan and heater element are designed to run together. It is fitted with two safety devices. Should an overheat situation occur the automatic device will operate. This leaves the fan running but turns off the element. If the cause, usually a temporary air obstruction, is removed the heater will reset itself and continue to operate. Should the cause remain a second reset will operate and turn the unit off. This device is to be manually reset and a qualified electrician should be consulted. Warning non user serviceable.

GENERAL MAINTENANCE ON ELECTRIC HEATERS

BN Thermic Electric Heaters are factory assembled and tested and are non user serviceable.

They are designed to operate for many years, problem free.

All moving parts are factory lubricated and should require no service.

Maintenance is limited to the keeping of both inlet and exhaust areas free from any accumulation of dust and other foreign materials. This is especially important immediately before a heating season. Any dust build-up can be loosened with a brush and a vacuum cleaner applied to the airways to ensure complete removal of dust materials. An unusual odour may be noted on the initial turn-on period as any dust particles may have been disturbed and will be burnt off from the element.

If you are in any doubt contact your electrician for advice.

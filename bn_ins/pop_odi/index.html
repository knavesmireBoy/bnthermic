ODI Draught Barrier Heaters

INSTALLATION AND OPERATING INSTRUCTIONS

ODI draught barriers are designed for horizontal mounting usually at low level below windows or glazed walls. They should be securely fixed to a wall, or to the floor using the optional pedestal feet. The power supply can be introduced through any of the four cable entries, making use of the full-length wireway if required. When the rear of the unit is visible, a smooth rear finish should be specified.

A minimum clearance of 300mm should be maintained in front of and above the unit. All installation work must be carried out by a qualified electrician and strictly in accordance with all the relevant regulations.

PACKAGING - Unpack the heater and remove all packaging material, ensuring all airways are clear and unobstructed.

LOCATION - The heater is intended to be wall mounted at floor level. Typical applications are for room or office heating, although under pew heating in churches is also effective. DO NOT position this heater under an electric outlet. DO NOT cover this heater or enclose it, or in any way restrict the free circulation of air around the heater. Ensure no curtains or any similar combustible materials get within 300mm of the heater.

WALL MOUNTING - The heater must be horizontally mounted, ensure any cables or conduits are securely attached ( if used ) and fix the heater to the wall using the holes in the back of the unit. Tighten the screws and then loosen by half a turn to allow for expansion of the case.

CONNECTIONS - Power entry is arranged to be on the left hand end, the terminal cover is retained Using 3 screws. Unscrew the terminal cover by removing the 3 screws. Prepare the incoming power leads and secure the earth cable coloured Green/Yellow to the green screw having the symbol . The live cable coloured Brown connect to the cable marked L. The Neutral cable coloured Blue should be connected to the cable marked N. THIS HEATER MUST BE EARTHED.

OPERATION - When energised the heater will provide maximum output. If an integral tamperproof thermostat is fitted the temperature range is 5°C - 35°C. To adjust the thermostat setting a small electricians screwdriver will be required. The thermostat is located on the right hand end of the heater, turning the screwdriver clockwise will increase the temperature and turning the screwdriver anti-clockwise will reduce the temperature. The heater is fitted with an overheat protection device. Should the heater register abnormal temperature rise anywhere along the heating element the device will switch the heater off. It will automatically reset when the element has cooled to normal operating temperature. If at any time you are in any doubt, consult your qualified electrician for advice.

THIS UNIT IS NON-USER SERVICEABLE.

GENERAL MAINTENANCE ON ELECTRIC HEATERS

BN Thermic Electric Heaters are factory assembled and tested and are non user serviceable. They are intended to operate for many years, problem free. All moving parts are factory lubricated and should require no service.

ENSURE THE SUPPLY TO THE HEATER IS COMPLETELY ISOLATED BEFORE COMMENCING. Maintenance is limited to keeping both the inlet and exhaust areas free from any accumulation of dust and other foreign materials. This is especially important immediately before a heating season. Any dust build up can be loosened with a brush and vacuum cleaner applied to the airways to ensure complete removal of dust and any debris. An unusual odour may be noted on initial power up period as any dust particles that may have been disturbed and will be burnt off from the element.

If you are in any doubt please consult your qualified electrician for advice.

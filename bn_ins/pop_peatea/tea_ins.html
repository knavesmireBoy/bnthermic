BN Thermic Instruction Sheet

TEA Portable Fan Assisted Heaters

INSTALLATION AND OPERATING INSTRUCTIONS

PACKAGING - Unpack heater and remove all packaging material. Ensure all airways are clear. Retain the instructions for future use.

LOCATION - TEA portable heaters conform to IP44. They must not be used in hazardous areas or in the immediate vicinity of baths, showers or swimming pools.
The heater must be placed on a steady and even surface. A minimum of 150mm clearance is required above and to each side of the heater. No object should be positioned within 2m of the outlet grille.

CONNECTIONS - The TEA range is provided with the following means of connection to the mains.
TEA321: 2m lead and 3-pin plug
TEA543: 2m lead and 16ACEE 5 round pin plug
TEA943: 16A CEE 5 round pin plug
TEA20: 2m lead with 63A CEE 4 round pin plug
TEA32: 2m lead (no plug)

Connection to the mains should be made in accordance with current regulations and protected by an appropriate fuse or circuit breaker.

OPERATION - All TEA heaters are fitted with an adjustable thermostat and a heat output selector switch. TEA heaters are protected by a manual-reset over-temperature protection device. Should this device operate the heater must be isolated and any foreign matter that may be obstructing the airways removed. Should the device operate repeatedly, turn off the heater and consult a qualified electrician.

GENERAL MAINTENANCE ON ELECTRlC HEATERS

Prior to commencing any periodic cleaning or servicing always ensure the supply to the heater is completely isolated.

BN Thermic Electric Heaters are factory assembled and tested and are non user serviceable.

They are designed to operate for many years, problem free.

All moving parts are factory lubricated and therefore should require no service.

Maintenance is limited to the keeping of both inlet and outlet areas free from any accumulation of dust and other foreign materials. This is especially important immediately before a heating season. Any dust build-up can be loosened using a brush and a vacuum cleaner applied to the airways to ensure complete removal of dust materials. When the heater is first turned on after cleaning an unusual odour may be noted this is due to the dust particles which inevitably will be disturbed and burnt off from the heating element.

If in doubt always consult a qualified electrician for advice.

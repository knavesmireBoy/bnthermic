BN Thermic Instruction Sheet

TP Thyristor Control Panels

INSTALLATION AND OPERATING INSTRUCTIONS

The panel is designed for wall mounting in a clean, dry environment where the ambient temperature does not exceed 30ºC.
A space of approximately 500mm should be left around the enclosure for heat dissipation.
Fix the enclosure to the wall using proprietary fixings. Remove gland plate drill and replace, and wire panel in accordance with the wiring diagram.
All wiring must comply with current regulations and be in compliance with the Health and Safety at Work Act. Building Regulations and BS7671.
Particular care should be taken to ensure that a neutral connection is made to each heater element and that this cable is correctly sized.
When cables are connected to the heater elements special high temperature cable or high temperature sleeving must be used.
Temperatures in the heater terminal boxes may exceed the safe limits of even high temperature p.v.c. (105ºC).
The sensor should be positioned out of direct sunlight or other heat sources approximately 1.5M up the wall.

A TP thyristor control panel is designed to supply a heater or group of heaters with a total load up to and including the stated maximum. When a panel is used with a smaller load, consideration should be given to changing the supply fuses to the control panel. This will ensure that adequate protection is provided for the cables between the panel and the heater.

For example a TP40 can be used with a maximum load of 40kW, which, on a 400V 3 phase supply, equates to 58 Amps per phase. When the TP40 is used with a 20kW load, the current rating would be reduced to 29 Amps per phase on a 3 phase supply.

If in doubt refer to BS7671 to check cable sizes.

SWITCH ON AND TEST PROCEDURE

OPEN DOOR WITH ISOLATOR IN ‘OFF’ POSITION

  1. With the door open ensure panel is securely fixed in a safe position.
  2. Check all wiring especially cable sizes and in heater terminal box ( high temperature sleeved). Ensure a neutral wire is connected to all heater elements.
  3. Carry out electrical safety tests including earth loop impedance and insulation resistance and record results.
  4. Check all safety switches and interlocks are correctly set.
  5. Check sensor is correctly positioned.
  6. Close door with isolator in ‘off’ position.
  7. Turn door isolator switch to ‘on’ position. ‘Panel Live’ indicator will illuminate.
  8. Switch heater on. Heater indicator will illuminate. Check PID Is set at correct temperature (see manual).
  9. Check heater is giving correct output.
  10. Ensure end user is familiar with controls.

PROBLEM SOLVING

PROBLEM: No Panel Live indicator illuminated.
SOLUTION: Check: electrical supply - door isolator is on - MCB in panel is in ‘on’ position. Investigate reason for failure.
PROBLEM: No Heater Run indicator illuminated.
SOLUTION: Check: heater is switched on.
PROBLEM: PID control display not illuminated.
SOLUTION: Check Heater Run indicator is illuminated - Panel safety stat has operated. Check panel

ROOM TEMPERATURE SENSOR

This is a black bulb room temperature sensor specifically designed to measure the temperature of radiant heating systems. The sensor housing is wall mounting and made from black flame retardant polycarbonate.

Features:

  • Aesthetically designed housing for radiant heating systems.
  • Available with all temperature measurement elements.
SPECIFICATION
Material Body: Flame Retardant Polycarbonate
Sensing Elements: PT100
Accuracy: ±0.2°C @ 70°C PT100
Operating Temperature: -10 to + 70°C
Terminals: 1.0mm recommended 2.5 max.
Location: Wall mounted 1.5M from floor level

SPECIFICATION

  • Epoxy painted steel enclosure - dimensions
  • Door interlocked isolator.
  • Individual switches and run indicators for heater.
  • Panel live indicator.
  • Individual MCB for output.
  • Mains input: 400v 50hZ 3 phase and neutral: or 230v 50hZ 1 phase.
  • Sensor: PT100 thermistor type with 10 metres or cable suitable for wall mounting.
  • Cable entry to panel: In top of enclosure via gland plate.

CAUTION

Always ensure panel is electrically isolated before carrying out adjustments or maintenance.

All panels are supplied in epoxy painted steel enclosure to IP21. Door isolation is provided as standard for safety. Output is thyristor control with P.I.D. control. A safety contactor and M.C.B. Protection are included, and built into each panel. All panels will dissipate heat from the thyristors via built in cooling fans in the front door of each panel. When siting the panel it is necessary to allow for the heat dissipation.

Typical figures are:
TP15 = 90 watts dissipation
TP40 = 245 watts dissipation
TP80 = 490 watts dissipation

Under no circumstances should the panels be mounted outside. Maximum ambient temperature 30°C.

BN Thermic Instruction Sheet

HWP-1500 Weather Resistant Halogen Heater

INSTALLATION AND OPERATING INSTRUCTION

The following information should be considered when installing HWP-1800 Halogen Infrared Heaters The heaters should always be installed using the brackets provided to ensure adequate clearance from the wall.

A safety distance of 1m should be left in front of the heater.

Heaters must not be installed in areas where hazardous vapours may be present as defined in BS5354.

The safety guard is only intended to prevent large objects directly hitting the lamp. Some applications may necessitate additional precautionary measures.

The heater conforms to IP55 and is thus suitable for outdoor installation.

Should the power cable be found to be damaged the heater must be returned to the supplier.

Installation Installation should be carried out by a qualified electrician and in accordance with current electrical regulations.

The heater must be protected by a suitably rated isolator and fuse or type 3 (type C) mcb.

The heaters must be mounted horizontally using the mounting brackets provided.

A minimum gap of 600mm should be allowed between the top of the heater and the ceiling or roof.

Be sure that the heater is not facing the ceiling or a flammable surface.

The heater should be mounted a minimum of 2200mm above ground level.

Ensure that no flammable material could come into contact with the heater.

Commissioning Perform insulation and earth continuity tests.

Switch on heater and ensure that the lamp is operating.

Ensure that the heater aims its warmth at the target area by adjusting the heater angle as necessary and tightening the retaining bolt when a satisfactory angle is achieved.

Check that the lamp remains in a horizontal plane.

The user should be made aware of all aspects of operation and safety and advised to retain these instructions for reference.

In unoccupied premises it is recommended that the heating system is switched off and isolated from the electrical supply.

Maintenance Halogen heaters contain no moving parts and therefore very little maintenance is required other than to ensure that there is no excessive build-up of dust or dirt on the reflector, emitter or fitting. To clean the heater, turn off and disconnect from the mains supply and, after allowing the heater to cool, wipe with a damp cloth.

The heater should be inspected at least once every year. More frequent inspection may be required depending on site conditions.

The halogen lamp is not field replaceable.

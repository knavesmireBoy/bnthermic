Quartz Infrared Outdoor Heaters

The following information should be considered when installing Quartz Infrared Heaters.

Installation should always be carried out by a qualified electrician or a competent person in accordance with current electrical regulations, protected by a suitably rated isolator and fuse or type3 mcb.

The heater should be mounted horozontally using the pole provided.

A minimum air gap of 400mm should always be allowed between the top of the heater and the ceiling or roof.

A safety distance of 1mtr should always be left in front of the heater.

Heaters should not be installed in areas where hazardous vapours may be present as defined in BS5345

This fitting is rated class I and IP65 suitable for outdoor use. To maintain the protection rating all conections to the mains must be done with a minimum of IP65 equipment.

Ensure that there is no possibility of inflammable material coming into contact with the heater, or is too near to the heater.

The essential element of the heater is the halogen filled quartz linear lamp with a tungsten element. This operates at about 1600°C and emits short wave infrared radiant heat. The lamp operates at full output almost instantly and emits a radiant energy of 1.4microns.

Quartz heaters are recommended by The Electricity council and Building Services Research Association for General space heating, Zone heating and localised spot heating

This product conforms to EU Directive 2002/96/EC.

This appliance bears the symbol of the crossed waste bin. This indicates that, at the end of its useful life, it must not be disposed of as domestic waste, but must be taken to a collection center for waste electrical and electronic equipment. It is the user's responsibility to dispose of this appliance through the appropriate channels at the end of its useful life. Failure to do so may incur the penalties established by laws governing waste disposal.

INSTRUCTIONS SHOULD BE CAREFULLY READ BEFORE INSTALLATION AND RETAINED BY THE USER.After unpacking ensure that the appliance is complete and shows no signs of visible damage or tampering.

Keep packing materials, which are potential dangers, away from children.

Before connecting to the mains ensure that the voltage is the same as indicated on the device.

Do not touch the heater with wet hands;

The heater should only be used with adult supervision.

Avoid improper use of extension cords.

Keep connections dry.

If the appliance is not used for a long period, disconnect it from the power supply.

The heaters are fitted with safety guards. These are intended only to stop large objects from hitting the emitters. On no account should the heater be operated with the guard removed.

The heating circle reaches a distance of about 4-6 meters around the appliance (Fig.10).

The essential element of the heater is the halogen filled quartz linear lamp with a tungsten element.

Positioning the Heater

The fitting should be placed on a level and flat surface.

The unit should be mounted at a minimum of 1800 mm above ground level.

A minimum air gap of 400mm should always be allowed between the top of the heater and a ceiling or roof (Fig.9).

Be sure that the heater is not facing the ceiling or inflammable substances.

A safety distance of not less than 1000mm should always be left in front of the heater.

Ensure that there is no possibility of inflammable material coming into contact with the heater, or lies underneath the heater.

Heaters should not be installed in areas where hazardous vapours may be present.

The appliance must not be located directly below a wall socket.

The heater must be installed so the switches can’t be touched by someone in a shower or bath.

If there is a risk of the heater being knocked, it must be secured to the floor with suitable mounting screws through the base (Fig.5).

Installation

Remove the 9 screws in the cover that has the rating label attached to it (Fig.4).

Depending upon how the fitting will be used the cable can be fed in 1 of 4 ways.

The cable is fed from the top down through the pole and out of the bracket on the base as per Fig 2

The cable is fed through the bracket on the base (Fig 3) then through the pole and out the top of the fitting.

The cable is fed from the top down through the pole and out through the base.

The cable is fed through the base, through the pole and out the top of the fitting.

Methods 3 & 4 must only be used if permanently mounting the fitting by screwing it to the ground!

The fitting is wired so it can be connected to the mains by single phase or three phase connection (3 lives + neutral + earth)

To wire the fitting single phase (220 – 240v), connect the Live wire to the terminal marked L1, Connect the neutral wire to the terminal marked N and the earth wire to the terminal marked       (Fig.7)

To wire the fitting three phase (380 – 415v) remove the link wires Marked A on fig.7 and connect the 3 live phases to L1 L2 and L3, connect the neutral wire to the terminal marked N and earth wire to the terminal marked     (Fig.8)

The power cord must be clamped using the cable grip.

The fitting must be earthed.

Now replace the top cover using all 9 screws(fig.4) NOTE:- All 9 screws must be used to ensure the IP rating.

Assembling

Join together the the two poles and secure with the 2 screws supplied (Fig.1).

Insert the assembled poles into the base and tighten  the screw until the poles are secure (pic.2).

Slide the heater onto the top pole and tighten the 3 screws until secure (Fig.3).

Use

Every lamp can be switched on with its own switch (fig.6). When pressing the switch avoid touching any metal parts. All heaters can be rotated to ensure that the heat is aiming at the correct angle by twisting the handle on the end of each fitting (fig.6). All users should be made aware of all aspects of operation and safety. In unoccupied premises it is recommended that the heating system is switched off.

Cleaning

Before any cleaning or maintenance is performed ensure that the heater is disconnected and isolated from the power supply. The heaters contain no moving parts and therefore very little maintenance is required other than to ensure that there is no excessive build-up of dust/dirt on the reflectors or lamps as this can cause overheating and premature lamp failure. To clean the appliance, wipe with a damp soft cloth.

Inspection

If the fitting is used as a portable heat source the mains cable must be regularly inspected to ensure it is not damaged in any way. If damage is found the fitting must not be used and the mains cable replaced by a qualified electrician.

Lamp replacement

The lamps used in the HWP fitting are robust and if knocks and shock vibrations are avoided, will last for around 5000 hours. For replacment contact your supplier.

BN Thermic Instruction Sheet

EP Series Panel Convector Heaters

INSTALLATION AND OPERATING INSTRUCTIONS

PACKAGING - Unpack the heater carefully and remove all packaging material ensuring that all open areas are unobstructed, the disposal of the packaging material should be made in accordance with any local regulations concerning waste. Remove and retain these instructions for future use.

LOCATION - Uses are for general space heating and therefore must not be installed or used in areas containing flammable and/or explosive materials or excessive dust or in the immediate surroundings of a bath, shower or swimming pool. Do not cover the heater as this can cause overheating and may damage the heater and could result in risk of fire.

POSITIONING - This range of heaters is intended for low level mounting, for optimum performance the heater should be mounted with the base of the heater not more than 600mm or less than 150mm from the floor. The heater must not be installed immediately below a fixed socket outlet. For optimum performance the heater should be away from opening doors and at the recommended height and air should be allowed to circulate freely around the heater.

WALL MOUNTING - Always ensure that the heater is mounted level and with the air-vent at the top. The bracket should be removed from the back of the heater and securely fixed to the wall. The heater is then snapped into place.

CONNECTIONS - Connections to the mains should be made by means of a suitable plug or other switching device in accordance with the current regulations and protected by an appropriate fuse or circuit breaker. Connect the live wire (coloured brown) to the terminal marked L and the neutral wire (coloured blue) to the terminal marked N. The third wire (coloured black) is for use with a pilot wire control system. Assuming such a system is not being employed, the wire should be insulated and left unconnected. THE BLACK WIRE IS NOT AN EARTH WIRE. This is a Class II heater and DOES NOT REQUIRE AN EARTH CONNECTION.

CONTROLS - Two thumb-wheels are located at the top of the unit. The thumb-wheel on the left should be set to 'A' to switch the heater 'off' and to 'M' to switch the heater 'on'.
The thumb-wheel on the right is to adjust the setting of the electronic thermostat. The highest setting is 9 and the lowest setting is 1. The setting marked * is a frost-protection setting and is commonly used when a room is to be left unoccupied for a period of time.
The two pegs fixed in the thumb-wheel can be removed and positioned in the holes in the back of the thumb-wheel to limit the adjustable range.

GENERAL MAINTENANCE ON ELECTRIC HEATERS

Prior to commencing any period cleaning or servicing always ensure the supply to the heater is completely isolated BN Thermic Electric heaters are factory assembled and tested they are non user serviceable.

The heaters are intended to operate for many years, problem free.

Maintenance is limited to keeping both the inlet and outlet areas free from any accumulation of dust or any other foreign materials. This is especially important immediately before the heating season. Any dust build up can be loosened using a brush and a vacuum cleaner applied to the airways to ensure completed removal of any dust.

When the heater is first turned on after cleaning an unusual odour may be noted, this is due to the dust particles which inevitably will be disturbed and will be burnt off from the heating element.

If in any doubt always consult a qualified heating and ventilation engineer / electrician for advice.
